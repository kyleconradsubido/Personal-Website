Projects

These projects reflect the kind of work I enjoy most: combining systems, data, teamwork, and business thinking to solve real problems. Since I am still a student, many of my strongest examples come from coursework, but they still show how I approach analysis, structure, and communication.

Tracking Gatorade Sales Using SQL

Focus: Database design, data integrity, and business analysis

This project involved working with normalized relational tables and using SQL concepts to keep data clean and reliable. My team focused on how structured data could support better reporting and better decisions. We also created visualizations and presented recommendations in a way that a larger audience could understand.

What I learned: Clean data matters a lot. When data is organized correctly from the beginning, reporting becomes more accurate and decision-making becomes more useful.

Improving Equinix Audit Time Using ServiceNow

Focus: Workflow automation, dashboards, and process improvement

This project looked at how automation and better workflow design could reduce the amount of time spent on audits. The idea was to replace repetitive manual work with a more efficient process supported by real-time updates and dashboards. I liked this project because it connected business value with system design in a very direct way.

What I learned: Process improvement is not only about speed. It is also about clarity, communication, and making it easier for teams to trust the information they are using.

Team Collaboration

Presentation Skills

Why These Matter
